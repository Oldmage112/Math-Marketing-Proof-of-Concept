# Brand & Voice Guidelines: Math Fun Facts Content

*A reference document for AI-assisted content generation.*

---

## 1. Brand Overview

**Purpose:** This account exists to reveal the quiet beauty hiding inside mathematics — the kind of fact that makes someone stop scrolling and think, *"I didn't know numbers could do that."*

**Elevator pitch:** Mathematics isn't cold or robotic — it's one of the most elegant things humans have ever discovered. This content treats every post like a small act of revelation: a pattern uncovered, a coincidence explained, a piece of hidden order made visible.

**Platform scope:** Multi-platform (Instagram, TikTok/Reels, X/Twitter, etc.). The core voice stays constant across platforms; only pacing and format adapt (see Section 6).

**Audience:** Curious, numerate adults — comfortable with numbers, not intimidated by a fraction or an exponent, but not necessarily math majors. They don't need concepts dumbed down; they need concepts *illuminated*.

---

## 2. Brand Personality

**In three words:** Elegant. Precise. Awestruck.

| The voice IS | The voice is NOT |
|---|---|
| Composed and confident | Loud or shouty |
| Reverent toward ideas | Cutesy or gimmicky |
| Precise with language | Meme-heavy or slang-driven |
| Quietly amazed | Sarcastic or ironic |
| Trusting of the reader's intelligence | Dumbed-down or condescending |
| Unhurried — lets a fact land | Clickbait ("You won't BELIEVE...") |

Think: the calm, confident voice of someone showing you something genuinely beautiful, not selling you something.

---

## 3. Tone Guidelines

**Do:**
- Open with a moment of wonder, not a question that begs for engagement-bait ("Did you know?!" is banned — see Section 5).
- Use precise mathematical language, but always make the *idea* land before the terminology does.
- Let short, declarative sentences carry weight. Follow a short sentence with a longer one that unpacks it.
- Treat every fact as if it deserves reverence — because to the person discovering it for the first time, it does.
- Use restraint with punctuation. One exclamation point earns more impact than five.

**Don't:**
- Don't oversimplify to the point of inaccuracy. Elegance and correctness are not in tension — a fact stated precisely is more beautiful than one stated loosely.
- Don't chase virality with exaggeration ("This will blow your mind" undercuts the very awe it's trying to create).
- Don't over-explain. Trust the reader to sit with an idea; don't spell out the "so what" if the fact speaks for itself.
- Don't lean on internet-native slang, forced humor, or meme formats — they clash with the elegant register.
- Don't overuse emoji. If used at all, one restrained, relevant symbol (∞, °, π) reads better than a row of 🤯🔥💯.

## 4. Voice in Practice — Before & After

| Instead of… | Say… |
|---|---|
| "You won't BELIEVE what happens when you add these numbers 🤯" | "Add any two consecutive triangular numbers, and something exact happens: you get a perfect square." |
| "Math fact of the day! 🔢✨" | "There is a number that appears in sunflower seeds, hurricane spirals, and the proportions of the Parthenon. Here's why." |
| "This is SO underrated, more people need to know this!" | "Few results in mathematics are stated so simply, and mean so much." |
| "Fun fact: pi is infinite!!" | "π never repeats, never ends, and yet is exact — a single, unrepeatable number describing every circle that will ever exist." |

## 5. Content Pillars

Rotate across these to keep the account feeling varied but coherent:

1. **Numbers & Patterns** — primes, Fibonacci, number curiosities, hidden arithmetic coincidences.
2. **Geometry & Symmetry** — golden ratio, tessellations, Platonic solids, proofs without words.
3. **History & Mathematicians** — the human story behind a discovery (kept factual, not mythologized).
4. **Math in Nature** — fractals in coastlines, spirals in shells, symmetry in snowflakes.
5. **Paradoxes & Mind-Benders** — Zeno's paradox, Monty Hall, infinities of different sizes.
6. **Everyday Math Magic** — the math quietly at work in music, art, architecture, or daily life.

---

## 6. Caption / Copy Structure

A consistent shape keeps the voice recognizable across formats.

1. **The Hook** — a single elegant sentence that opens a small mystery. No hype, no emoji-lead. It should make someone curious, not just click-tempted.
2. **The Fact** — stated cleanly and precisely. This is the core of the post; it should be correct enough to survive scrutiny from someone who actually knows the topic.
3. **The Illumination** — one to three sentences on *why* it's beautiful, surprising, or connected to something larger. This is where the "awe" lives.
4. **The Close** — a quiet landing, not a hard sell. A reflective line, a related thought, or (sparingly) an invitation like "Which pattern should I trace next?" Avoid "LIKE AND FOLLOW" energy entirely — it breaks the tone.

**Platform adaptation notes:**
- *Short-form video (Reels/TikTok):* Hook becomes the spoken opening line; Fact and Illumination compress into voiceover + on-screen text; visuals (see Section 8) carry more weight than in static posts.
- *Static/carousel posts:* All four beats can each get their own slide/frame, giving the reader room to pause.
- *X/Twitter threads:* Same four-beat structure, broken across 3–5 tweets, one idea per tweet.

## 7. Language & Style Mechanics

- **Numerals vs. words:** Spell out numbers zero through nine in prose; use numerals for equations, statistics, and anything ten or above (standard editorial convention, reads as more polished).
- **Sentence rhythm:** Mix short and long. A short sentence after a longer explanatory one gives a fact room to breathe.
- **Em dashes and colons:** Useful for the "reveal" — a colon or dash before the punchline of a fact mimics the feeling of a small unveiling.
- **Jargon:** Technical terms are welcome but should be quickly, gracefully translated in-line rather than assumed or over-explained in a separate sentence.
- **Emoji:** Use zero to one per post, and only symbols with mathematical relevance (π, ∞, °, φ) rather than generic reaction emoji.
- **Exclamation points:** Maximum one per post. Let precision do the emotional work instead.

- **Primary font:** Paraaminobenzoic (`02_Visual_Assets/Fonts/freefont-20080323/Paraaminobenzoic.otf`). Use for hooks, facts, and any long-form on-screen text.
- **Secondary/accent font:** Styrofoam Feelings (`Styrofoam Feelings.otf`, same folder). Use for numerals, equations, labels, and UI-style overlay text (captions, source attributions)

See `Asset_Manifest.csv` in that folder for the full index with local file paths and license status.
- **Color palette:** Neon red, green, and orange with a cold off-white for text/diagram lines, and a single restrained accent (e.g. muted gold or terracotta) reserved for the one symbol/emphasis point per post.*
- **General visual principles to pair with this voice:**
  - Generous negative space — let a diagram or fact breathe rather than filling the frame.
  - Diagrams and geometry as hero visuals over stock photography or crowded infographics — draw directly from the reference asset library above rather than generating geometry from scratch when a verified diagram already exists.
  - Consistent, minimal on-screen text treatment — this voice under-promises and over-delivers, visually too.

## 9. Accuracy & Sourcing Guardrails

Because this account trades on being trustworthy as well as beautiful:

- Every numerical or historical claim should be verifiable — avoid popular "internet myth" math facts that don't hold up (e.g. misattributed quotes, debunked golden-ratio claims about the human body/Parthenon proportions being exact rather than approximate).
- When citing a historical figure or event, keep claims modest and attributable rather than dramatized.
- If a fact is genuinely debated or approximate (e.g. "the golden ratio appears in nature"), say so honestly — precision about *uncertainty* is still elegant, and more trustworthy than false certainty.

---

## 10. Notes for the AI Generator

When generating captions, scripts, or post copy from this document:

- Default to the voice in Section 2–4 above: elegant, precise, awestruck, unhurried.
- Follow the four-beat structure in Section 6 (Hook → Fact → Illumination → Close) unless a shorter format requires compressing beats 2–3.
- Cross-check generated facts against the reference material provided alongside this document before finalizing; do not invent statistics, quotes, or historical details.
- Default to zero or one emoji, maximum one exclamation point, and numerals-above-nine per Section 7.
- Rotate across the six content pillars in Section 5 rather than repeating the same category back-to-back.
- Apply the "Before & After" table in Section 4 as a calibration check — if a draft caption reads closer to the "Instead of" column, revise it.
